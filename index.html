First-Time Albania Essentials
Social vibe Warm and direct. A friendly greeting goes a long way.
Tipping Not mandatory, but 5–10% appreciated in restaurants.
Cash culture Carry small notes for buses, beach spots, and local cafes.
Dress Casual is fine; cover shoulders/knees for religious sites.
Water Use bottled water in coastal areas to avoid stomach issues.
Road rhythm Traffic can be assertive; cross carefully, especially in Tirana.

Day-to-Day Etiquette
Cafe rhythm Coffee breaks are social time. Slower service is normal, not bad service.
Meal timing Lunch starts later and dinners often begin after 19:30, especially in cities.
Restaurant check Ask for the bill directly ("llogarinë, ju lutem"), it may not come automatically.
Beach basics Many beach clubs charge for umbrella + loungers; confirm price first.
Photography Fine in public, but ask before close portraits and avoid military/police sites.
Quick Travel Safety
Emergency number 112
Taxi Confirm price before ride if meter is unclear.
Bus travel Arrive 30–40 min early; schedules can shift.
Cards vs cash Always keep both. South coast can be cash-heavy.
